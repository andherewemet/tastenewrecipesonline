Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups arborio or paella rice
  • 500g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 150g chorizo, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp saffron threads, soaked in 2 tbsp warm water
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh parsley and lemon wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Chorizo:
    • Heat olive oil in a large pan.
    • Add chorizo slices and cook until browned. Remove and set aside.
  2. Cook the Vegetables:
    • In the same pan, sauté onions, garlic, and bell pepper until soft.
    • Add tomatoes and cook until they break down.
  3. Toast the Rice:
    • Stir in the rice and toast for 1-2 minutes.
    • Pour in white wine and let it evaporate.
  4. Simmer:
    • Add broth, smoked paprika, and saffron water.
    • Stir once, then let cook uncovered on low heat for 15 minutes.
  5. Add Shrimp and Chorizo:
    • Nestle the shrimp and chorizo into the rice.
    • Continue cooking for 5-7 minutes until shrimp is pink.
  6. Serve:
    • Garnish with parsley and serve with lemon wedges.

Tips:

  • Avoid stirring after adding the broth for an authentic crust (socarrat).
  • Use seafood stock for a richer flavor.

Ingredients:

For the Chicken Marinade:
  • 500g boneless chicken, cubed
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
For the Sauce:
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2 cups crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    • Mix all marinade ingredients in a bowl and coat the chicken.
    • Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours (overnight is best).
  2. Cook the Chicken:
    • Heat oil in a pan and cook the marinated chicken until browned. Set aside.
  3. Prepare the Sauce:
    • Melt butter in a pan, sauté onions until golden.
    • Add garlic, ginger, and cook until fragrant.
    • Pour in crushed tomatoes and spices, simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Finish the Dish:
    • Add heavy cream and sugar, stirring well.
    • Return the chicken to the pan and cook for 5 more minutes.
  5. Serve:
    • Serve hot with basmati rice or naan, garnished with cilantro.

Tips:

  • Adjust spice levels to taste.
  • For a smoky flavor, grill the marinated chicken before adding it to the sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 200g mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 tbsp flour
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Cooked egg noodles or rice

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Beef:
    • Slice the beef thinly against the grain for tenderness.
    • Season with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika.
  2. Cook the Beef:
    • Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium-high heat.
    • Add the beef and sear for 2 minutes per side. Remove and set aside.
  3. Sauté the Vegetables:
    • In the same pan, add butter and let it melt.
    • Sauté onions until soft, then add garlic and mushrooms. Cook until mushrooms are golden brown.
  4. Make the Sauce:
    • Sprinkle flour over the mushroom mixture and stir for 1 minute.
    • Slowly add beef broth while stirring to avoid lumps.
    • Mix in Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, and sour cream. Stir well.
  5. Combine:
    • Return the beef to the pan and simmer for 5 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  6. Serve:
    • Serve over egg noodles or rice, garnished with fresh parsley.

Tips:

  • Use high-quality beef for the best texture.
  • Adjust the thickness of the sauce with more broth if needed.
